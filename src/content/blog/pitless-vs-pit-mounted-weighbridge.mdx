Choosing between a **pitless** and a **pit-mounted** weighbridge is one of the
first decisions when planning a truck-weighing installation. Both weigh
accurately; they differ in how they are installed and maintained.

## Pitless weighbridge

A pitless weighbridge sits above ground on a raised foundation with approach
ramps. There is no pit to excavate, so installation is faster and civil costs
are lower. The open underside makes cleaning, inspection, and load-cell service
straightforward.

## Pit-mounted weighbridge

A pit-mounted weighbridge is set flush into a pit so the deck is level with the
surrounding ground. It saves yard space and avoids ramps, which suits sites
where vehicles approach from multiple directions — but it requires excavation
and good drainage.

## How to choose

- **Limited space / flush access needed:** pit-mounted.
- **Faster, lower-cost install and easy maintenance:** pitless.
- **High groundwater or drainage concerns:** pitless is often simpler.
